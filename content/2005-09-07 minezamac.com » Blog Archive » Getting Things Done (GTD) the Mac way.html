Getting Things Done (GTD) the Mac way

Well I expect this will be the first post of many regarding my latest effort in organizing my life and workplace. Saturday I was plunking around late at night and stumbled upon a reference to GTD in a blog post somewhere. Initially thinking it was an expletive gone awry I dismissed it, then seeing it again I did a search for it’s meaning. I have gone through the 7 habits for highly retentive people, and gotten palmed, pocketpc’d and daytimer’d but I have high hopes for this new one. It seems to think a bit more like I do anyway, so hopefully it will be an improvement. I am currently using iCal and NathanBowers GTDTddlyWiki a GTD oriented version of JeremyRuston’s Open Source TiddlyWiki. If you have never worked with a Tiddlywiki it is quite the experience, first of all it is a client side non-linear (it’s the word he uses) personal web notebook. It really is something quite extraordinary in regard to being able to do so much with only a browser and an html webpage. Check it out, quite useful tools really. Anyway, I’ll keep you posted with my progress and my configuration, I have considered using Circus Ponies Notebook and am not sure I won’t but I just really like the idea of a file you can keep on a thumb-drive or ipod and use anywhere, now get it to run on the ipod and my goodness, i’m there. Already 50 pages into David Allen’s Book “Getting Things Done” after picking it up yesterday at lunch, pretty pleasant read thus far and the paperback is cheap enough. I’ll let you know more when I’m through it and have started using it, or that I chucked it all and your copy is available in the heap.
