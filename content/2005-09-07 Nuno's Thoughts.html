Getting Things (Un)Done

I was just talking to a friend on Skype who had bought Getting Things Done (the book) some time ago. At the time he was really excited about how the book had taught him how easy it is to remain organised under increasing pressure, and how he couldn't wait to put some of what he had learnt in practice.

Six months on and there we were chatting, when I asked if the method had been working out for him. His answer was that "yeah... kind of", but he had slipped back to a life of chaos since he didn't have the energy to pursue all the little tricks to keep him on the right path. The funny thing is, I asked the question expecting precisely that answer.

You see, anyone who is tempted by a book on self-organisations is bound to be the type of person who feels disorganised all the time. They are called ENFP's (buying the book has never crossed my mind by the way - I don't feel disorganised). In fact, the person in question admited to being an ENFP - meaning he enjoys disorganisation and chaos because he strives on creativity. So, do I think everyone who is employing GTD or running GTD TiddlyWiki is the creative type ? Probably not - I am sure there are lots of people out there who bought the book who couldn't be creative if their lives depended on it. But I am frankly convinced that the majority of individuals who do buy into this kind of thing do so without realising that it is a dead end. If you think you need the book in the first place it is very likely it will not do a bit of good for you because it is just not who you are.

My advice to these people is that they should tackle the problem in a different way - instead of spending all the energy trying to reshape your way of doing things, apply it instead in redirecting what you actually do. We can't all be David Allen.

If you think I am talking out of my arse just try this - ask the nearest PA, clerical worker or accountant what their method of self-organisation is. If I am right they will tell you they have one, but will be totally incapable of defining what it is once you push for an answer beyond a few sentences - they can't because it is just who they are, not what they do.
